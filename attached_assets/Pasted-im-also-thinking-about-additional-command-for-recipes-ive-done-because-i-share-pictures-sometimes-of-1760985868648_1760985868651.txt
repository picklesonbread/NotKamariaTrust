- cinnamon rolls

- cheese star

Ingredients:

Yeast dough
-500 g wheat flour
-14 g instant yeast (2 packets) or 50 g fresh yeast
-50 g sugar
-200 ml milk
-2 eggs
-50 g butter

Filling
-300 g curd cheese (quark)
-1 egg
-1 packet vanilla sugar
-50 g sugar
-40 g butter
-optional ‚Äì 50 g candied orange peel

Also
-1 egg for brushing the dough
-powdered sugar for dusting or glaze: 1/2 cup powdered sugar + 2 tablespoons orange or lemon juice + optional 1 tablespoon candied orange peel

Preparation

Yeast dough
If using fresh yeast, prepare a starter: mix the yeast with a tablespoon of sugar until dissolved, then add 100 ml of warm milk and a tablespoon of flour. Mix well and set aside for 10 minutes to rise. Instant yeast can simply be mixed with the flour.

Place the flour in a bowl, add the yeast, the remaining milk, and sugar, and gently mix. Add the eggs and mix slowly again.

Add the softened butter and begin kneading the dough. Knead for about 15 minutes, until smooth and elastic. Cover and let rise for about 45 minutes.

Filling
Place the curd cheese (quark) in a bowl, add the egg, vanilla sugar, sugar, and diced butter. Mix for about 1 minute. Optionally, stir in the candied orange peel.

Shaping the dough
Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead briefly. Form a ball and divide it into 4 equal parts.

Roll out the first piece into a circle about 25 cm in diameter. Place it on a large baking tray lined with parchment paper. Spread 1/3 of the cheese filling over it, leaving about a 1 cm border around the edge.

Roll out the second piece of dough and place it on top of the first layer. Spread with another third of the filling, then place the third layer of dough on top. Spread with the remaining cheese and cover with the final circle of dough.

Cut the dough into 16 sections, but do not cut all the way through to the center (leave the middle uncut), as shown in the photos below.

Twist each section twice, alternating directions ‚Äî twist one piece twice to the right, and the adjacent one twice to the left. Pinch the ends of each pair together. Let the shaped dough rise for about 20 minutes.

Preheat the oven to 180¬∞C. Brush the top of the dough with a beaten egg mixed with 1 tablespoon of milk. Place in the preheated oven.

Bake for about 25 minutes, until golden brown. Remove from the oven, let cool slightly, and then dust with powdered sugar or drizzle with glaze (powdered sugar mixed with juice).

Notes
